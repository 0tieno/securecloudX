Research Charter
Building Trusted Cybersecurity Intelligence for Better Decisions
Executive Statement

Cybersecurity has become one of the defining risks of the digital age. Every government, financial institution, business, university, healthcare provider, and citizen increasingly depends upon secure digital systems to operate safely, efficiently, and with confidence.

While cyber threats continue to evolve rapidly, decision-makers often lack objective, locally relevant, evidence-based research that enables informed strategic decisions.

Many existing publications focus on individual incidents, vendor-specific intelligence, or global trends. Few provide an independent, comprehensive, and transparent assessment of cybersecurity resilience within the Kenyan context.

SecureCloudX Research was established to help bridge this gap.

Our mission is not merely to report cyber incidents, but to transform fragmented information into trusted intelligence that supports better governance, stronger resilience, more effective policy, and more informed investment decisions.

Every SecureCloudX publication is guided by rigorous research principles, transparent methodologies, and a commitment to public value.

Our Vision

To become Africa's most trusted independent cybersecurity research institution, producing evidence-based intelligence that strengthens digital resilience, informs national policy, and supports executive decision-making.

Our Mission

To produce world-class cybersecurity research that enables governments, regulators, boards of directors, executives, researchers, and security professionals to make informed decisions through objective analysis, transparent methodologies, and actionable recommendations.

Telecommunications.

Yet questions remain:

How resilient are our critical sectors?
Which threats are increasing?

Purpose of This Charter

This charter establishes the governance principles, research standards, ethical framework, and publication methodology that guide every SecureCloudX Research publication.

It ensures consistency, transparency, credibility, and accountability across all research activities.

Research Philosophy

Our work is founded upon one central belief:

Better cybersecurity decisions require better evidence.

We believe cybersecurity research should:

Inform rather than persuade.
Analyze rather than speculate.
Educate rather than sensationalize.
Build resilience rather than assign blame.

Our objective is to strengthen ecosystems—not to identify winners and losers.

Guiding Principles
1. Independence

SecureCloudX Research operates independently of commercial vendors, political interests, and organizational influence.

Research findings shall never be altered to satisfy sponsors, partners, governments, or private organizations.

Independence is fundamental to credibility.

2. Objectivity

All conclusions shall be supported by verifiable evidence.

Researchers shall distinguish clearly between:

Observed facts
Analytical interpretation
Expert opinion
Forecasts
Assumptions

Readers should always understand which is which.

3. Transparency

Every report shall clearly disclose:

Research methodology
Data sources
Inclusion criteria
Validation process
Assumptions
Limitations

Transparency enables trust.

4. Accuracy

Every reasonable effort shall be made to verify published information through multiple reliable sources.

Where uncertainty exists, uncertainty shall be explicitly acknowledged.

5. Evidence First

Claims shall not be made without sufficient supporting evidence.

A compelling narrative shall never outweigh factual accuracy.

6. Public Value

Research should contribute positively to cybersecurity maturity, national resilience, and public understanding.

Research Ethics

SecureCloudX Research commits to the highest standards of research integrity.

Researchers shall:

Avoid conflicts of interest.
Correct errors promptly.
Respect confidentiality.
Attribute all external work appropriately.
Avoid plagiarism.
Avoid sensationalism.
Never fabricate or manipulate data.

Integrity is non-negotiable.

Research Methodology Framework

Every research project shall follow a standardized lifecycle.

Phase 1 – Research Design
Define research question.
Define objectives.
Define scope.
Identify stakeholders.
Develop methodology.
Establish inclusion criteria.
Phase 2 – Data Collection

Information may be collected from:

Regulatory publications
Government reports
Annual reports
Academic literature
Security advisories
Vendor intelligence reports
Public disclosures
Court records
Incident reports
Publicly available datasets
Interviews (where applicable)
Phase 3 – Validation

Information shall be:

Cross-referenced.
Verified.
Classified.
Documented.
Assigned confidence levels.

No significant conclusion shall rely upon a single unverified source where corroboration is reasonably possible.

Phase 4 – Analysis

Researchers shall identify:

Patterns
Trends
Relationships
Emerging risks
Sector-wide observations
Lessons learned

Analysis should distinguish correlation from causation unless supported by evidence.

Phase 5 – Peer Review

Major publications should undergo independent technical and editorial review before publication.

Reviewers may include:

CISOs
Security engineers
Academics
Policy experts
Legal professionals
Industry specialists

Peer review strengthens quality; it does not transfer responsibility for the final content.

Phase 6 – Publication

Reports shall include:

Methodology
Limitations
References
Supporting evidence
Version number
Publication date
Phase 7 – Continuous Improvement

Research does not end at publication.

Where new evidence emerges:

Corrections shall be issued.
Datasets updated where appropriate.
Future editions refined.
Evidence Standards

Sources shall be evaluated according to reliability and relevance.

Illustrative hierarchy:

Primary sources (official statements, regulatory filings, court judgments, standards)
Peer-reviewed research
Official organizational reports
Reputable industry reports
Credible journalism
Independent technical analyses

Conflicting evidence shall be discussed rather than ignored.

Typical fields include:

Attack vector

Affected assets

Customer impact

Source references
Confidence level
Analytical Standards

SecureCloudX Research seeks to answer:

What happened?
Why did it happen?
What patterns exist?
What lessons emerge?
What decisions should leaders consider?

The purpose is not to assign fault, but to extract insight.

Neutrality Policy

SecureCloudX Research does not rank organizations based on incomplete or confidential information.

Reports shall avoid language such as:

"Bank X has poor cybersecurity."

Instead:

"Based on publicly available evidence, this report observed differences in public disclosure practices and incident response communications across institutions."

Language matters because credibility depends on precision.

Handling Sensitive Information

SecureCloudX Research will not knowingly publish:

Confidential information obtained unlawfully
Personal data without a lawful basis
Information that could unnecessarily increase operational risk for organizations
Technical details that materially facilitate malicious activity

Research should contribute to resilience, not exploitation.

Research Limitations

All reports shall clearly acknowledge their boundaries.

For example:

Findings reflect publicly available evidence.
Undisclosed incidents may exist.
Public reporting quality varies.
Some observations may change as new information becomes available.

Transparency about limitations strengthens confidence in the conclusions.
